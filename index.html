Historical Research Project

Womens and LGBT Japan Informational Website created to demonstrate the lack of rights and social acceptance

Women in Japan were recognized as having equal legal rights to men after World War II and LGBT sexual relations has been legal in Japan since 1880, however rights and social status for women and LGBT remain unbalanced compared to men or hetersexual counterparts. The highlight area for this website is the history of women and LGBT within Japans society and culture and how womenâ€™s rights and LGBT rights have been impacted by society and historical events. This will include achievements made by both women and LGBT to continue towards the path of human rights, the many ways in which women and LGBT people are attempting to bring awareness and educate Japan on these issues, and the response of Japans society and how their opinions have changed over the years.

Japan although historically accepting and empowering LGBT and women have become westernized to the point where Japan has dramatically forgotten about its past and the achievements made by LGBT and women for the future of Japan.
